# AIS_datathon_2026

## Problem Statement
To what extent still the American Dream attainable?
- Is the American Dream attainbale across the U.S. (statewise)?
- Which states are growing the fastest?
- Are incomes rising with GDP?
- Are some regions falling behind?
- Which industry will most likely achieve the American Dream?
- How likely does people in tech achieve the American dream?

The BEA website allows you to look at the economy from many different angles. Depending
on the direction of your analysis, you may explore questions such as:
- How is the U.S. economy growing overall?
- Which industries are contributing the most to GDP?
- Are some sectors creating more value and opportunity than others?
- How is consumer spending changing over time?
- What do personal income patterns suggest about financial well-being?
- Are certain states, regions, or local areas performing better economically than others?
- How do inflation-related indicators affect affordability and quality of life?
- Does economic growth appear broad-based, or concentrated in specific industries and places?


The phrase “American Dream” can mean different things, but in economic terms it is often
linked to ideas like:
- stable income
- rising opportunities
- access to growth
- affordable living
- economic mobility
- long-term financial security
The BEA data can help you investigate whether today’s economy supports these conditions.
For example:
- GDP data can show whether the economy is expanding.
- GDP by Industry can reveal which sectors are driving growth and where opportunities
may be strongest.
- Personal income data can help you examine whether people are actually benefiting from economic growth.
- Consumer spending data can offer insight into household confidence and purchasing power.
- Regional and local data can help you compare whether opportunity is equally available across places.

This means your analysis can move beyond opinion and become a data-backed exploration of
whether economic progress is translating into real opportunity for people.

## Definition
American dream 
-  Anybody, from any background, can be successful and follow their dreams with enough hard work and perseverance.

- Starting a family in a nice town, with enough financial success to be generally free from worry. The traditional idea of "making it". 

- Better in life than previous generations.
